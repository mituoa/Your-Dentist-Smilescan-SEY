## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are dental practices and doctors managing their public presence, patient intake, team follow-up, and internal operations. Secondary users are patients landing on practice pages and judging trust, seriousness, and clinical quality within seconds.

### Brand Personality
Timeless, premium, and composed. The brand should feel like an enduring house label rather than a fast-moving startup. It should read as credible, calm, editorial, and subtly luxurious without becoming fashion-theatrical or tech-generic.

### Aesthetic Direction
Neutral master-brand architecture anchored by `Your Dentist`, with sub-brands that feel like product labels inside one heritage system. Visual language should stay restrained, high-trust, and European-editorial: quiet typography, measured geometry, meaningful symbols, and colors that feel durable rather than trendy.

### Design Principles
1. Every section name should feel like a lasting label, not a software feature tab.
2. Symbols should be simple enough to age well and strong enough to work as marks, favicons, and navigation badges.
3. The family should feel related through structure and typography, while each product owns a distinct semantic symbol and accent color.
4. Public-facing surfaces should feel calmer and more refined than internal workflow surfaces.
5. Avoid generic SaaS language whenever a clearer, more enduring word can carry the role.
